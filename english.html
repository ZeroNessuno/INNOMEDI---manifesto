In the Name of

Poetic Manifesto

We live within the narration of a century of progress,
we tell ourselves that humanity has never been more humane.
But photos, reports, bodies
recount another historical reality:
useless explosions, boundless destruction,
the use of a force we can no longer rationalize.

While the epilogue of this false order
consumes itself in the deconstruction of colonial peripheries,
here we are — so similar to Them —
holding the guarantee of a dignified life
spent upon the skin of outsourced, invisible slaves.

It is our duty to doubt, and to choose:
this is Freedom
while systemic cognitive dissonance blinds us.

How can these worlds coexist?

Adonai Tzva'ot / Our Father

[Once the animal has been drained of blood, let it burn.
The essence of the sacrifice shall rise to heaven.]

A rescue vehicle
gutted on the asphalt,
metal sheets, bent ribs,
the road’s throat mute.

A blackened limb yields to oxygen,
exhales a thin smoke;
what remains is only a body of incense,
a military prayer.

[Lord of hosts
Thy kingdom come
Thy will be done]

Bereshìt / Origin

It is an ecstasy of hate:
a cry, a flame,
the crowd a pack,
a single creed.

And the genetic doubt
synthesizes a fear
implanted, epidermic,
a defense reaction,
autoimmune.

[We will teach you to distinguish
the anatomical traits of the tribe]

Hitgalut / Revelation

I find a hypothetical landscape,
fractures, anomalous geometries.
No form is recognizable,
no form remains
whole when the sky
falls.

The dust of concrete
is the silence of peoples,
the suffocating slag,
revenge — white phosphorus inheritance;
the trace that will not fade
and unbinds the flesh,
melts the bones,
scars the eyes,
indelible.

[We will build houses, cinemas, malls
on the unstable ground of bodies,
upon the filled mass graves]

As global injustice turns into entertainment and mass distraction, the rights once conquered are being silently compressed.
We are the state, we must decide.

It is time for revelation, for the final act:

DO YOU WANT TO DIE OR BE REBORN?
